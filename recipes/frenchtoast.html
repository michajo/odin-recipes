Mac and Cheese

A super quick and easy French Toast for busy mornings!

No need to wait until you have stale bread around.
This fast and easy French Toast recipe will hit the spot before school or even as a dinner treat!

Ingredients

  • 1 Pound Dried Macaroni - use bronze cut for the best flavor and texture
  • 2 Tbsp Butter - you can use any neutral oil if you'd like
  • 2 Tbsp Flour
  • 2 Cups Whole Milk
  • 6 Slices (4 oz) of American Cheese - this helps bind all the cheeses together
  • 1 Cup (4 oz) Cheddar Cheese, shredded - grate your own, as pre-shredded cheese has starch in it which interferes with melting
  • 1 Cup (4oz) Munster Cheese, shreded
  • 1 Cup (3oz) Parmesan Cheese, shredded and divided in half
  • 1 Cup Panko Breadcrumbs - unseasoned
  • 1 tsp Garlic Powder
  • 1 tsp Onion Powder
  • Salt and Freshly Ground Black Pepper - to taste

Directions

Cheese Sauce

  1. Heat large sauce pan over medium head
  2. Add the butter to melt
  3. Stir in Flour
  4. Cook for 1-2 Minutes until the raw flour smell is goine - you aren't looking to brown it
  5. Slowly pour in the milk a little at a time while mixing well - a whisk works well for this
  6. Add the Garlic and Onion Powders
  7. Bring to a low simmer while continuing to stir - you dont want to fully boil this
  8. Once you see small bubbles around the eges, turn off the heat
  9. Stir in the cheeses until completely homogenious
  10. Add Salt and Pepper to Taste

Assemble

  1. Preheat oven to 350° F
  2. Boil 4 Quarts of salted water in a large pot
  3. Cook Macaroni about a minute less than the box directions - make sure it is al dente, you dont want mac and cheese mush
  4. Drain Pasta and return to pot
  5. Carefully stir in Cheese Sauce
  6. Once Combined, Transfer to a 9x14 Casarole Dish
  7. Spread Evenly
  8. Sprinkle the top with Panko and remaining 1/2 Cup Parmasean Cheese
  9. Bake uncovered for 20-30 minutes until top is a nice light brown
  10. Remove and let cool for about 10 minutes before serving
  11. ENJOY!
